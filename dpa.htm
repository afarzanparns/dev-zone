Data Protection Act (2018)

The Data Protection Act affects how Heaven Exists uses your personal information.



The Data Protection Act is the UK's implementation of the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).



If we use any personal data, then we shall obey strict rules, known as 'data protection principles'. We ensure that the information is:



  • used fairly, lawfully and transparently
  • used for purpose specified only
  • used in a limited way only for what is necessary
  • accurate
  • held for no longer than required
  • held securely, namely protection against unauthorised access, processing, loss, destruction or damage




There is more specific protection for information deemed to be sensitive, namely:

  • race
  • ethnic background
  • political view
  • religious belief
  • membership of trade union
  • genetic code
  • biometric data
  • health
  • sex life or orientation




Legal right


Under the data protection act, you have the right to learn what information Heaven Exists stores about you.



See what data that we have about you


If you wish to see what data Heaven Exists holds about you, then please send the request in writing.



How long it should take

Heaven Exists is required to provide a copy of your data within a month.

Sometimes, Heaven Exists may need more than a month to supply data. In this case, Heaven Exists shall advise you -



  • within a month of your request
  • reason for a delay
